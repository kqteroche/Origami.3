Origami Instructions and Diagrams


Step by step diagrams are probably the most popular and easiest way to follow how to fold things out of paper. Trying to find good instructions on the Internet is a lot of work though. To help your search, we've put together a large database of free origami diagrams anywhere on the internet.

Camel

  1. Camel's ears are furry
  2. Camel can move easily across the sand because of its specially designed feet
  3. When they find water, they drink as much as possible.

Chameleon

  1. Chameleon's are reptiles like iguanas.
  2. Changing skin color helps them communicate
  3. They can wrap their long tails to tree branches.

Pigeon

  1. Pigeon's eat seeds, fruits, and plants.
  2. Columbidae is a bird family consisting of pigeons and doves. It is the only family in the order Columbiformes.
  3. These are stout-bodied birds with short necks, and short slender bills that in some species feature fleshy ceres.

Panda

  1. The giant panda, also known as the panda bear, is a bear native to South Central China.
  2. It is characterised by its bold black-and-white coat and rotund body.
  3. The name "giant panda" is sometimes used to distinguish it from the red panda, a neighboring musteloid.

Flying Cicada

  1. Cicadas, unlike other Auchenorrhyncha, are not adapted for jumping (saltation).
  2. They have the usual insect modes of locomotion, walking and flight, but they do not walk or run well, and take to the wing to travel distances greater than a few centimetres.

Teddy Bear

  1. A teddy bear is a stuffed toy in the form of a bear.
  2. Developed apparently simultaneously by toymakers Morris Michtom in the U.S. and Richard Steiff under his aunt Margarete Steiff's company in Germany in the early years of the 20th century, and named after President Theodore "Teddy" Roosevelt, the teddy bear became a popular children's toy, celebrated in story, song, and film.

Butterfly

  1. Butterflies are insects in the macrolepidopteran clade Rhopalocera from the order Lepidoptera, which also includes moths.
  2. Adult butterflies have large, often brightly coloured wings, and conspicuous, fluttering flight.

Crane

  1. Cranes are a family, the Gruidae, of large, long-legged, and long-necked birds in the group Gruiformes
  2. The 15 species of cranes are placed in three genera, Antigone, Balearica, and Grus
  3. Unlike the similar-looking but unrelated herons, cranes fly with necks outstretched, not pulled back.
